Terrorist Attacks in 2017

This visualisation is meant to give a general overview of terrorist attacks in 2017. It focuses on the types of terrorist attacks, the weapons used in the attacks, the targets of the attacks and the suicide rates of the attacks.

The map above displays all the terrorist attacks in 2017 by their latitude and longitude. It is meant as an overview of the general attacks occur and where they are most concentrated. As can be seen, most attacks are concentrated in three locations, Central Africa, the Middle East and South Asia. The colour of each dot represents whether it was a suicide attack, meaning whether the perpertrator(s) died by their own hand. Blue means it was not a suicide, whereas red means it was. The size of the each dot represents the number of deaths due to the attack. As can be seen, most attacks results in minimal deaths, with a few outliers, such as the attack with the highest death total of 588.

Attack types

The following graph shows the terrorist attacks per month separated by the type of the attack per month.
By far the most prevalent attack type is Bombing/Explosion.
Whereas the least prevalent attack type is Hijacking.
The month with the most terrorist attacks is May, with almost 1100 attacks.
Whereas the month with the least is December, with around 750 attacks.

Weapons

The following graph shows the types of weapons used in terrorist attacks, with a total of 5465 attacks.
This shows that Explosives are the most common weapon for terrorist attacks. Although this could be caused by the fact that Explosives are the most common weapon by far in Bombing/Explosion attacks, the most common attack type.
This is followed by Firearms, with a total of 3145 attacks, due their versatility, as they appear in all attack types except Unarmed Assault, Bombing/Explosion and Unknown.
The least common weapon is Fake Weapons which appeared in a single Hostage Taking (Kidnapping) attack.
The next least common is Sabotage Equipment, which appeared solely in Facility/Infrastructure Attack attacks.

Targets

The following graph shows the targets of terrorist attacks.
This shows that Private Citizens & Property is the most common target for attacks, with a total of 2945 attacks. This is because it is at least the second most common target in every attack type.
The second most common target is Military, with a total of 2352 attacks. This is because it is the most common target in Armed Assault and Unknown and the second most common target in Bombing/Explosion.
The least common target is Abortion Related, appearing in only one Bombing/Explosion attack.
The next least common target is Other, with only 7 attacks.

Suicides

The following chart shows the rate of terrorist attacks that end in suicide. This is when the perpertrator(s) died by their own hand.
Blue means it was not a suicide, whereas red means it was. As can be seen, very few attack actually resulted in a suicide, with only 844 attacks from the total of 10897 attacks, making around 8% of the total attacks.
In fact, for most attack types less than 10% of attacks result in a suicide.
In fact, a few types have no suicides associated with them, those being Facility/Infrastructure Attack, Hijacking and Unknown.
The major exception is Bombing/Explosion attacks, which has the highest suicide rate of nearly 16%.
